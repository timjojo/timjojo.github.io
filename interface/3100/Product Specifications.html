3. Product Specifications

3.1 Functional Specifications

3.1.1 Number of board/card

255 boards/cards (max.) (Up to 16 boards/cards of the same model may be controlled.)

3.1.2 Number of Channels

Up to the sum of channels of the boards/cards installed on the system.

3.1.3 Data Transfer Mode

  • Programmed I/O
  • FIFO
  • Memory
  • Bus master

3.1.4 Number of Samples

1 through 230 (*4)
3.1.5 Sampling Rates
Data Transfer Mode Sampling Rate
Programmed I/O 0.01 Hz to 10 kHz (*2)
FIFO 0.01 Hz to 400 kHz (*2)(*3)
Memory 0.01 Hz to 300 kHz (*3)
Bus master 0.48 Hz to 10 MHz (*2)

3.1.6 Trigger Capabilities

  • Analog trigger
    (positive, negative, positive/negative, out of the range, and into the range)
  • External trigger
  • External trigger with mask using general purpose digital input pins

3.1.7 Trigger Timing

Sampling start-trigger and sampling stop-trigger are available.

3.1.8 Start-Trigger Delay Capabilities

Available samples for post-trigger: 1 through 230 (*4)
Available samples for pre-trigger: 1 through 230 (*4)

3.1.9 Stop-Trigger Delay Capabilities

Available samples for post-trigger: 1 through 230 (*4)

3.1.10 Data Processing

  • Averaging
  • Interpolation
  • Data conversion: from binary to physical value and vice versa
Notes: Maximum ratings and specifications depend on the board/card specifications and operating environments, and other conditions.
*2 This sampling rate is the value when the sampling is finished on only one channel. When two or more channels are sampled simultaneously, the sampling rate is reduced as follows:
Maximum sampling rate (multiple  simultaneous channels) = single channel sampling rate/number of sample channels.
*3 This sampling rate is the value when the number of the data greater than the size of buffer memories on the board/card are sampled. Otherwise, the sampling rate can satisfy what the board/card supports.
*4 These values depend on the board/card specifications. Please refer to "Attentions."
